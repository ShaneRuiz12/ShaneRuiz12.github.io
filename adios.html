"Mi Último Adiós," which translates to "My Last Farewell" in English, is a poem written by Dr. José Rizal, the Philippine national hero. It is considered one of his most significant works and was penned just before his execution on December 30, 1896. "Mi Último Adiós" serves as Rizal's final farewell to his beloved country, the Philippines, and to his fellow Filipinos. The poem reflects his profound love for his homeland and his unwavering dedication to the cause of Philippine independence. The poem is written in Spanish, the language of the colonial rulers, symbolizing Rizal's desire to reach a wider audience, both within and beyond the Philippines. It expresses his thoughts, emotions, and aspirations as he faces his imminent death. In "Mi Último Adiós," Rizal reminisces about the beauty of his homeland, the cherished memories he shared with his countrymen, and the struggles they faced under Spanish rule. He expresses his hope that his sacrifice will serve as a catalyst for change and inspire future generations to continue the fight for freedom. The poem is filled with poignant imagery, evocative language, and profound emotions. It is a reflection of Rizal's intellect, patriotism, and deep sense of justice. Through his words, he implores his compatriots to remember him, to never forget the cause for which he gave his life, and to continue the pursuit of liberation. "Mi Último Adiós" encapsulates Rizal's legacy as a national hero, a visionary, and a martyr. It is a timeless testament to his unwavering commitment to the Filipino people and their struggle for independence. The poem continues to be revered and celebrated as an embodiment of the indomitable spirit of the Filipino nation.
