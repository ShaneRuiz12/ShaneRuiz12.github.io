"Sa Aking Kabata" (To My Fellow Youth) is a poem attributed to Dr. Jos√© Rizal, the Philippine national hero. It is believed to have been written when Rizal was just eight years old, showcasing his precociousness and early awareness of social issues. The poem is a heartfelt message addressed to Rizal's fellow youth, expressing his hopes and dreams for a brighter future for the Philippines. It reflects his love for his country and his desire to see positive change. In "Sa Aking Kabata," Rizal encourages his fellow youth to embrace their cultural heritage and to love and respect their native language. He advocates for unity and cooperation among the youth, emphasizing the importance of standing together to achieve progress and overcome societal challenges. The poem also highlights the significance of education and knowledge in shaping the destiny of the nation. Rizal emphasizes the power of learning and the pursuit of wisdom, urging the youth to seek knowledge as a means to uplift themselves and their country. Despite its simple and youthful composition, "Sa Aking Kabata" carries a profound message. It serves as an early indication of Rizal's lifelong commitment to the Filipino people and his belief in the potential of the youth to effect positive change. While there is some debate about the authenticity of Rizal's authorship of the poem, its sentiments align with his later works and his dedication to the advancement of the Filipino people. "Sa Aking Kabata" continues to be celebrated as a poignant reminder of the importance of education, cultural pride, and unity among the Filipino youth. It serves as an enduring inspiration for generations to come, encouraging them to embrace their identity, work towards progress, and contribute to the development of their beloved nation.
